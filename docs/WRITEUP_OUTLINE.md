## Section 2 — Why this benchmark exists

Two paragraphs, no more.

Paragraph 1: existing Whisper benchmarks all measure either (a) raw model
throughput on one framework with no concurrent load, or (b) a vendor's own
serving stack on their own preferred hardware. Neither answers the question
production teams actually have: "given my workload shape, which framework
gives me the best dollars-per-audio-hour at acceptable tail latency?"

Paragraph 2: that's what this is — five frameworks, two GPUs, five concurrency
levels under a closed-loop streaming workload, with full reproducibility.

---

## Section 3 — What we measured

Bullet list (the one place bullets earn their keep — readers want to scan
this):

- **Model:** OpenAI Whisper-large-v3, the latest fully open-weights Whisper
- **Eval audio:** LibriSpeech test-clean utterances concatenated to ~30s
  pseudo-clips (50 clips × 3 iterations = 150 requests per cell)
- **Frameworks:** HF Transformers (baseline), faster-whisper, vLLM, Ray Serve,
  NVIDIA Triton (Python backend)
- **GPUs:** A100 SXM 80GB (premium), L4 (cost-effective)
- **Concurrency:** 1, 8, 32, 64, 128 closed-loop workers
- **Metrics:** latency p50/p95/p99, aggregate RTF, WER, GPU utilization, peak
  GPU memory, cost-per-audio-hour at on-demand RunPod pricing

50 cells total. Every cell ran with 10 sequential warmup requests excluded
from timing, then 3 iterations through the eval set. Median across iterations
is the headline number; standard deviation is in the JSON.

## Section 4 — What we deliberately didn't measure (and what's coming)

Honest about scope. Each "didn't" is a hook for a v1.x follow-up:

- **Triton with TensorRT-LLM compilation.** v1's Triton cells are Python

  follow-up in v1.2.
- **Long-form audio and variable-length workloads.** The streaming cells use
  uniform 30s pseudo-clips. Whisper's chunked-decoding behavior on real long
  audio is where most benchmarks lie, and v2 addresses that head-on.
- **Model variants.** distil-whisper, large-v3-turbo. v2.1.

We're naming what we didn't do so that critics can't claim we hid it. Each is
a planned post; the cadence is published below.

## Section 7 — Methodology in 90 seconds

A single section that hits the four most-defensible methodology choices, with
a "for the full set, see [docs/METHODOLOGY.md]" pointer. The four:

1. **Closed-loop concurrency** (N active users, not Poisson arrivals) — match
   the actual ASR usage pattern.
2. **Identical text normalization across frameworks** via Whisper's own
   EnglishTextNormalizer — different frameworks emit different default
   punctuation, and unnormalized WER comparisons are not meaningful.
3. **Warmup excluded, iterations median** — the discipline that distinguishes
   benchmark from microbenchmark.
4. **Failures are findings** — OOM, framework refusing a config, etc. are
   reported as cell outcomes, not omitted from the matrix.
