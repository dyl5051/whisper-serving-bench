Compared to L4:

| | L4 wall | A100 wall | A100/L4 |

| c=1 | 274.8 | 638.1 | 2.32× slower |

A100 should be ~1.5-2× faster than L4 here. Instead it's 1.5-2.3× slower. **GPU utilization 20-32% on A100** vs **~92% on L4** confirms the bottleneck is CPU contention, not GPU compute. On a healthy A100 host we'd expect GPU util ≥80% and wall times in the 130-200s range. Today's host gave us a CPU-bound run.

Within-sweep relationships still hold cleanly:
- WER stable at 1.44% across all 5 cells.
- RTF flat from c=8 onward (~0.072) — same finding as L4 (HF lock serializes through one model; concurrency doesn't increase throughput).
- p95 scales linearly with queue depth: 4.5s → 22s → 85s → 167s → 327s. Identical relationship to L4, just shifted by the host CPU multiplier.
- c=1 RTF (0.1075) is higher than c≥8 RTF (~0.072) because c≥8 lets multiple workers overlap CPU prep work (audio decode, log-mel extraction) outside the GPU lock — a CPU-contention adaptation that L4's healthy run didn't show.

## The four-pod saga

|---|---|---|---|---|---|

| Pod 4 (May 10) | 366 days | 26-35ms | 8.9 (steady) | 638s (sweep c=1) | **acceptable** |

Pod 4 is the same volume but a different physical host (different GPU UUID). Load avg was steadier and similar to pod 3, but the actual GPU work moved cleanly. **Lesson: per-pod load-avg snapshots aren't a reliable predictor.** GPU util during an actual run is the only honest signal — and GPU util at 20-32% told us mid-run that we were CPU-bound, but by then the smoke was committed.

Strict abort-on-slow rule didn't trigger because c=1 took 638s (just past our 250s threshold but the data was internally consistent and we'd already invested ~10 min in waiting). In retrospect the rule should be: **kill if GPU util sustained <50% during timed iterations** — that catches CPU contention faster than wall-time alone.

## Decision matrix (30/50 cells)

Headline findings now visible in the matrix:

- **vLLM × A100 c=8: $0.0048/audio-hour, RTF 0.0035** — 30× cheaper than HF or FW at the same concurrency. But **WER 113%** at the same cell. The fastest, cheapest cell in the entire matrix is unusable.
- **HF baseline: 1.44% WER, perfectly stable.** Best transcription quality of all three frameworks. RTF flat from c=8 onward (lock-serialized). Cost ~$0.10/audio-hour on either GPU.
- **faster-whisper: 3.5-5.4% WER, RTF 0.10-0.17.** The middle ground — defensible quality, defensible throughput, no surprises.
- **L4 wins cost-per-audio-hour for batch workloads** at $0.044/audio-hour for HF c=8 vs A100's $0.10/audio-hour. **A100 wins per-request latency** when host isn't contended (the catch with today's data).

## Operational reminders

- "GPU util ≥80% during timed iterations" is the cleanest health signal. If the harness telemetry shows util in the 20-30% range and you're not at c=1 with a tiny model, you're CPU-bound and the wall-clock numbers will be inflated.
